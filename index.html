Scan to BIM Services: Point Cloud to BIM and Revit Conversion

A scan to BIM service, also called a point cloud to BIM service, converts laser-scan point cloud data into an accurate as-built Revit model of a building as it stands, ready for AEC and facility teams to renovate, retrofit or document it. The factors that decide the quality of a point cloud to BIM model are accuracy against the source point cloud, turnaround speed, and compliance with the target LOD. Reviewing a scan to BIM provider's QC standards, track record and client reviews is essential when choosing a scan to BIM service.

ViBIM is a scan to BIM and point cloud to BIM company that models all four disciplines, architecture, structure, MEP and topography, with 250,000+ modeling hours since 2014 for survey and AEC firms across the US, UK, Canada, Australia and parts of the EU.

Every point cloud to BIM project produces as-built drawings, BIM models, custom Revit families or facility-management data, modeled discipline by discipline and coordinated into one federated as-built. The scan to BIM conversion service follows a six-phase, 13-step process in Autodesk Revit, ReCap and Navisworks, with in-house Revit API automation that keeps turnaround faster than the market average. Scan to BIM work runs from RCP/RCS, E57 and registered point cloud data into native .RVT, .IFC and .DWG with no on-site visit, and two independent QC layers verify every model against the source point cloud to ISO 19650, PAS 1192 and BIM Forum LOD at the level your project needs. Completed scan to BIM projects range from commercial complexes and hospitals to heritage churches and transport infrastructure, each modeled for renovation, retrofit or as-built records. Each scope is quoted in 12 to 24 hours, hourly or as a lump sum, and new clients start with a free trial.

How Scan to BIM Conversion Works

Scan to BIM conversion at ViBIM runs a six-phase, 13-step process that turns your registered point cloud data into a coordinated, parametric as-built BIM model. Point cloud goes in; a parametric as-built model comes out, with two independent QC layers acting as the gate before anything ships. Scan to BIM modeling runs the same way regardless of which scanner produced the data, and no on-site visit is needed. The six phases of the scan to BIM conversion process are set out below:

1 Project Setup

Project setup begins your scan to BIM conversion by confirming scope, target LOD, deliverable formats and coordinate system, then ingesting and registering your point cloud data. We agree these parameters up front so every later phase models to the right specification. There is no on-site visit; we work directly on the point cloud you send, whatever scanner produced it.

  • 1Project Brief Analysis & Scope Clarification
  • 2Point Cloud Preparation & Coordinate Setup — the coordinate system is the origin every element is positioned against, so all trades align
  • 3Level & Grid Establishment
  • 4Element Classification & Modeling Setup

Output: a registered, coordinate-correct point cloud with levels and grids ready for modeling.

2 Discipline Modeling

Discipline modeling converts the registered point cloud into parametric Revit geometry for each trade, architecture, structure and MEP, built to the agreed LOD. Elements are modeled directly from the registered point cloud so the as-built reflects real conditions. Architecture frames the space first, then structure and MEP are placed to it, because each trade carries its own scope, LOD and output format.

  • 5Architecture Modeling — parametric: components carry editable parameters, not dead linework
  • 6Structure & MEP Modeling — if required

Scope detail for each trade lives on the discipline pages above.

Output: coordinated parametric geometry for each trade at the agreed LOD.

3 Validation & Families

Validation and families reviews modeled geometry for LOD and LOI compliance and builds the custom components the project needs. The model is checked for completeness and consistency before it moves to coordination.

  • 7LOD & LOI Validation — LOD is the Level of Development, the geometric detail of an element; LOI is the Level of Information, the non-geometric data attached to it such as labels, materials and specs. They are two separate measures.
  • 8Family Creation & Parameter Setup

Output: an LOD- and LOI-validated model with the parametric families the project needs.

4 Coordination & QC

Coordination and QC runs clash detection between disciplines and puts the model through ViBIM's two-layer independent quality check against the source scan. This phase is where accuracy is confirmed, with two separate reviewers verifying the model before it advances.

  • 9Model Coordination & Clash Check — catching where elements from different trades collide
  • 10QA/QC Review & Tolerance Compliance — the two independent QC layers against the source point cloud

Scan to BIM Accuracy, LOD & Quality Standards

Scan to BIM accuracy at ViBIM is controlled through two-layer independent QC, ISO 19650 workflows and clearly defined Levels of Development, not a single tolerance figure. These three measures are how ViBIM controls model quality, and how you can judge a provider before outsourcing; each is set out in the headings below. ViBIM establishes quality by checking back against the source point cloud and following ISO 19650, PAS 1192 and BIM Forum LOD, rather than by quoting one number.

The three measures behind scan to BIM quality are set out below

Accuracy & Tolerance

Scan to BIM accuracy describes how closely the BIM model matches the real, scanned conditions, governed by source scan resolution and the modeling tolerance agreed for your project. Every element is verified against the point cloud through two-layer independent QC rather than quoting a blanket millimetre figure. Each element's position and geometry are checked against the source cloud, deviations from it are reviewed, and nothing is left missing or misaligned. The accuracy you reach depends on the quality of the input scan and the tolerance agreed for the project, so it is set per project rather than fixed across all of them.

Tolerance = the allowable difference between the model and the source scan points.

Quality Standards (ISO 19650 + QC)

Quality standards for scan to BIM combine ISO 19650 information-management practice with a two-layer independent scan to BIM quality control process, where two reviewers separately validate each model against the scan. This is how ViBIM brings scan to BIM models to delivery consistent, coordinated and audit-ready. Three standards anchor the work:

ISO 19650The international standard for managing BIM information.
PAS 1192The UK predecessor that ISO 19650 grew out of.
BIM Forum LODThe framework that defines how much detail a model carries.

The two independent QC layers check geometry, parameters, deviation, missing elements and consistency against the source point cloud, cross-checked to US and UK standards. The work is done by AEC-qualified engineers against independent US and UK checks.

Levels of Development (LOD 100–500)

Level of Development (LOD) combines Level of Detail (geometry) and Level of Information (data) to define how reliable a BIM model is, from LOD 100 (conceptual) to LOD 500 (field-verified as-built). We deliver LOD 100 to 500, with LOD 300 the most common for scan to BIM and LOD 350 used where MEP coordination is critical.

The LOD scale runs from conceptual to field-verified — each level shown by its 3D model below:

LOD differs from LOI (Level of Information), the non-geometric data attached to elements such as manufacturer or model number; the two are complementary. LOD is also not the same as Revit's Detail Level (Coarse, Medium, Fine), which only changes display, not the model's development. Scan to BIM Services: Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about ViBIM's scan to BIM services cover accuracy, formats, LOD, turnaround, pricing, NDA and the free trial.

Is scan to BIM a software or a service?
Scan to BIM is a service, not a software. It is the professional process of converting point cloud scan data into a parametric BIM model, delivered by modelers like ViBIM. Software such as Revit and ReCap is only the toolset our team operates; what you receive is a finished, QC-verified as-built model, not a software license.
How accurate are your point cloud to BIM models?
Point cloud to BIM models from ViBIM are verified element by element against your source scan through two-layer independent QC, so the model reflects real conditions to the resolution of your point cloud. Final accuracy is governed by scan quality (point density, coverage, registration) and the LOD you choose, with QC run to ISO 19650 and PAS 1192 under independent US and UK checks, rather than a single fixed tolerance.
What point cloud formats do you accept?
Point cloud formats we accept include all common types, RCP, RCS and E57, from any scanner or capture method. Keep each RCS under 5 GB per file, or send the dataset as a single .zip. We are scanner-agnostic with no on-site visit needed, and confirm compatibility before the project starts if your data is in another format.
What file formats do you deliver?
File formats we deliver are typically three: .RVT for native Revit workflows, .IFC for open BIM exchange (IFC2x3 or IFC4), and .DWG for 2D CAD sheets. We agree the deliverable formats during project setup so the model drops straight into your workflow.
How much do scan to BIM services cost?
Scan to BIM services cost depends on building size, LOD and deliverable type, not a flat per-square-foot rate. Each project is scoped individually on an hourly-rate or lump-sum basis, with a free trial model so you can confirm fit and pricing before committing; the cost drivers are broken down in the pricing section above. A scan to BIM project's turnaround depends on building size, LOD and deliverable type, driven by total modeling hours rather than a fixed calendar, with on-time delivery the norm. Which LOD do you deliver?
The LOD we deliver spans 100 to 500, at the level your project requires. LOD 300 is the most common for scan to BIM, LOD 350 supports MEP coordination, and LOD 500 gives a field-verified as-built. LOD (Level of Development) combines geometric detail and reliability; LOI (Level of Information), the non-geometric data, is set separately, and neither is Revit's display-only Detail Level.
Can I upgrade as-built CAD drawings to BIM later?
Yes. As-built CAD drawings can be upgraded to a full BIM model later, because both come from the same point cloud. ViBIM can deliver 2D as-built drawings now and rebuild them into a parametric Revit model from the identical scan whenever your project needs it, with no need to re-scan.
Do you sign an NDA and offer a free trial?
Yes. An NDA is signed on request, and a free trial model lets you assess scan to BIM quality before committing. The trial is a demo model built on your own sample data that also calibrates QC to your standards, and only your dedicated PM transfers files over secure channels (FTP, Box, Cintoo or ACC).
