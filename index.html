BIM Modeling Standards & Quality Control

Quality on an outsourced BIM project comes down to how the work is checked before it reaches you. At ViBIM, every BIM model passes two independent QC layers, cross-checked to both US and UK standards and verified element by element against the source scan before delivery. That review runs against the standards your market works to: ISO 19650 and PAS 1192 on the UK side, and the USIBD Level of Accuracy (LOA) specification and AIA documents (E202, E203, G202) on the US side. Models are delivered at the Level of Development your project sets, from LOD 100 to 500.

Across both QC layers, every model is checked on five points:

  • Geometry: each element's position and shape verified against the source point cloud.
  • Parameters: element data and properties checked for completeness and correctness.
  • Deviation: differences from the source scan reviewed and resolved within the agreed tolerance.
  • Missing elements: scanned features confirmed present, with nothing dropped from the model.
  • Data consistency: naming, units and structure kept consistent across the whole model.

Every model is checked to the BIM standards your market works to, on both the US and UK sides:

  • ISO 19650 and PAS 1192: UK BIM information-management standards.
  • USIBD LOA: US Level of Accuracy specification.
  • AIA E202, E203, G202: US BIM protocol and model-progression documents.

BIM Modeling Services: Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about ViBIM's BIM modeling services cover what Revit BIM modeling is, model accuracy, file formats, turnaround, cost, NDA and the free trial. These answers help architects, engineers and survey firms before starting a project. The most common questions are answered below:

What is Revit BIM modeling?
Revit BIM modeling is the process of converting point cloud, scan or drawing data into intelligent, parametric 3D models in Autodesk Revit that hold both geometry and information. A Revit BIM model carries data on every element, so it can be used for design, coordination, costing and facility management.
How accurate are ViBIM's BIM models?
BIM models from ViBIM are verified element by element against the source scan through two independent QC layers, so the model reflects real conditions to the resolution of your point cloud. Accuracy is governed by scan quality and the LOD you choose, with QC run to ISO 19650 and US and UK standards.
What file formats do you deliver?
File formats we deliver are typically three: .RVT for native Revit, .IFC for open BIM exchange, and .DWG for 2D CAD, set to your project's requirements. Deliverable formats are agreed during project setup so the BIM model drops straight into your workflow, and IFC is available in IFC2x3 or IFC4.
Do you sign an NDA and offer a free trial?
Yes. An NDA is signed on request, and a free trial model lets you assess BIM modeling quality on your own data before committing. Files move only through your dedicated PM, over secure channels.
How long does a BIM modeling project take?
A BIM modeling project's turnaround depends on building size, LOD and deliverable type, with on-time delivery the norm and a timeline confirmed against your scope up front. ViBIM returns a turnaround estimate within 12 to 24 hours of receiving your point cloud, and most projects run 1 to 3 weeks end to end.
